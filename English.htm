It is encouraging that the church has plans to build up this vision of Church as Family, and Family as Church. However, my personal view is that there is still a lack of zealousness from church leaders. Sometimes, I get the impression that the lay leaders are carrying out "instructions". They seem to be messengers carrying out perfunctory duties. With the convenience of what's app, messages are passed. However, there is no follow-up to take the extra step to find out why certain members do not wish to turn up for certain meetings. We need nurturing leaders. Not clinical doers. The Church has been organising different seminars and programmes. Sometimes, external speakers are invited. But there were occasions when members only know about it with only one week's notice. This is rather inefficient use of resources as busy Singaporeans have too many things on their plate. Even if they would have loved to attend the seminar, they have to give it a miss because they have already committed to something way ahead. When you have to pay for a invited speaker, and the turnout is miserable, this is not efficient use of resources. I feel that there are too many "last minute" invitations to meetings and events. Maybe we can have a calendar of events on a quarterly basis. Not just church-wide events but events which relate to specific groups too. This is because we cannot rule out that we may have interested parties who can also attend small group events. Thank you.
